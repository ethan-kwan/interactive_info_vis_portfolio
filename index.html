Sketch narrative

Context: This sketch is an ambient kitchen timer that merges an analog clock with the body of a kettle. It highlights how kettles usually take a few minutes to boil, and makes that waiting time visible and a bit more playful for someone brewing tea or coffee at the stove.

  • Design decisions: I turned the kettle into a clock face so the user can read time directly from the object they are waiting on. A visible fire under the kettle reinforces that the water is actively heating, and the second hand tracks real-world time so the user can see exactly how many seconds and minutes have passed.
  • Design decisions: Each time a full minute passes (as indicated by the seconds hand completing a full rotation), an extra burst of steam comes out of the spout. This ties a familiar visual cue—steam—with the more abstract notion of elapsed minutes, making the passage of time easier to feel and not just read.
  • Future work: I would like to improve the visuals with more detailed shading, textures, and smoother steam motion, and potentially add a start/stop button that lets the user “turn on” the heat so the clock and steam only begin once they intentionally start the boil.

Sketch narrative

Context: This sketch is meant as a calm, nature‑inspired way to glance at the current time of day. Instead of a traditional clock face, it uses a flower and the sun/moon to show time passing for someone working near a window or taking short breaks during the day.

  • Design decisions: I used the sun and moon crossing the top of the canvas to show the general time of day, mirroring the real sky from east to west. This makes it easy to understand whether it is morning, afternoon, or night at a glance.
  • Design decisions: The flower’s two leaves act as clock hands: the shorter leaf is the hour hand and the longer leaf is the minute hand. The layered petals around the center help visually anchor these hands while still keeping the overall feel organic instead of mechanical.
  • Future work: I would like to refine the gradients and textures so the sky, petals, and leaves feel more natural, and potentially add small hour/minute markers or hover labels so users can read exact times without losing the soft, nature‑focused aesthetic.
